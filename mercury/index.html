what is mercury in retrograde?

Several times a year, it appears as if Mercury is going backwards in the night sky. Because Mercury is the closest planet to the sun, its orbit is much shorter than Earth’s. When Mercury passes the Earth, we experience this illusion, known as a retrograde period.

Of all planetary aspects, this one seems to garner the most attention as this phenomenon is one of the few that affects everyone in a fairly uniform way, and its effects are usually pretty obvious.

why is it important?

When a planet retrogrades, astrologically it is in a resting or sleeping state. So while Mercury naps, the activities that it governs don’t have the benefit of a well-functioning, wide-awake planet to supervise them. These times are associated with confusions, delay, and frustration.

According to astrology, Mercury rules communication, travel, contracts, etc.

how does this effect me?

Things that can be affected while Mercury is in retrograde:

Miscommunications are abound.
It can be hard to get decisions from others.
Be extra careful when sending important documents and emails.
Confusion is abundant.
